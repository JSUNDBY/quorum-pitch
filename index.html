Most HOA boards run on a Gmail thread, a spreadsheet, and a prayer. Quorum replaces the clutter with one calm place for dues, meetings, announcements, and everything in between — designed for real people, not property managers with IT degrees.

The problem

A $2 billion market stuck in 2008.

Over 358,000 HOAs manage 40% of American homes. Most of them still run on Excel, Google Groups, and paper checks. The software that exists feels like tax prep — built for accountants, not neighbors.

358k

HOAs in America

Managing 74 million residents and over $100 billion in annual assessments. Almost none love their software.

14

Disconnected tools

A typical mid-size HOA juggles email, Dropbox, QuickBooks, a payment portal, Nextdoor, and a filing cabinet. Nothing talks.

62%

Volunteer-led boards

Most boards are neighbors donating evenings. Current software assumes full-time staff. It punishes the people running it.

One calm place. Four different people.

How it works

One filing cabinet. One tag on every drawer.

Under the hood, the whole product is a well-organized filing cabinet. Every piece of data — a charge, a meeting, a photo of a broken sprinkler — wears a tag that says which HOA it belongs to. That's how we keep hundreds of communities cleanly separated.

Why now

The timing is strange, and it's ours.

Four things just became true at the same time. Any one of them would make this easier. All four make it inevitable.

Boards are turning over.

The generation that ran HOAs on paper is aging out. The new boards expect software that works like Venmo, not like a 1998 tax return.

State laws are tightening.

California, Florida, Texas, Arizona, and Colorado have all passed new HOA transparency laws since 2023. Paper-and-email doesn't cut it anymore.

Payments got cheap.

Stripe Connect and ACH pull-through mean we can offer banking-grade dues collection without being a bank. The cost curve finally works.

Incumbents are bloated.

AppFolio and Buildium were built for property managers, not residents. Their apps feel like accounting software because they are. We're the thing underneath.

Let's make HOAs feel human again.

This is a deep, boring, gigantic market where no one has shipped anything that actually feels good. We can be the first.
